Historical Inversion Periods

Educational reference: What was happening during each yield curve inversion?

?? Aug 1978 - May 1980 (21 months)

Context: Stagflation crisis. Inflation hit 13.5% in 1980. Paul Volcker appointed Fed Chair (Aug 1979), began aggressive rate hikes.

What Happened: Double-dip recession (1980, 1981-82). Unemployment rose from 6% to 10.8%. Volcker's "Saturday Night Massacre" rate shock broke inflation but caused severe recession.

Austrian Perspective: Culmination of 1970s monetary expansion following Nixon abandoning gold standard (1971). Malinvestments built up during inflationary boom. Volcker's contraction liquidated unsustainable ventures. Painful but necessary correction.

?? Sep 1980 - Jul 1982 (22 months)

Context: Volcker kept rates high (Fed Funds peaked at 20% in June 1981). Crushing inflation at all costs.

What Happened: Deepest recession since Great Depression. Unemployment peaked at 10.8% (Nov 1982). Manufacturing devastated. Savings & Loan crisis began.

Austrian Perspective: Classic boom-bust cycle. Previous decade's easy money created widespread capital misallocation. High interest rates revealed which investments were only profitable due to cheap credit. The "Reagan boom" that followed was built on sounder foundation.

?? Dec 1988 - Nov 1989 (11 months)

Context: Fed tightening after 1987 stock crash recovery. Savings & Loan crisis escalating. Real estate bubble popping.

What Happened: Mild 1990-91 recession. 1,000+ S&L institutions failed. First Iraq War (1990-91). Tech stocks beginning to surge.

Austrian Perspective: S&L crisis rooted in 1980s deregulation without removing deposit insurance moral hazard. Easy money in early 1980s fueled reckless real estate lending. Inversion signaled that malinvestments (especially CRE) were unsustainable at higher rates.

?? May 1998 - Jul 1998 (2 months)

Context: Asian Financial Crisis (1997-98). Russia defaulted (Aug 1998). Long-Term Capital Management collapsed (Sep 1998).

What Happened: Brief inversion, no US recession. Fed cut rates ("Greenspan Put"). Dot-com bubble accelerated. Y2K fears.

Austrian Perspective: Fed's rescue of LTCM set dangerous precedent - "too big to fail" for hedge funds. Rate cuts to bail out overleveraged speculators inflated dot-com bubble. Moral hazard expanded. Short inversion correctly signaled systemic fragility, but Fed intervention prevented healthy liquidation.

?? Feb 2000 - Dec 2000 (10 months)

Context: Dot-com bubble peak (Mar 2000). Nasdaq topped at 5,048. Fed had raised rates to 6.5% to cool speculation.

What Happened: 2001 recession. Dot-com crash (Nasdaq fell 78%). 9/11 attacks (Sep 2001). Enron, WorldCom accounting scandals. Fed slashed rates to 1%.

Austrian Perspective: Textbook malinvestment boom in tech sector. Years of easy money (post-LTCM) channeled into unprofitable internet companies valued on "eyeballs" not earnings. Inversion signaled unsustainability. Greenspan's 1% rates after crash planted seeds for housing bubble.

?? Dec 2005 - Jun 2007 (18 months)

Context: Housing bubble peak. Subprime lending explosion. CDOs, MBS securities proliferating. Fed raising rates after years at 1%.

What Happened: 2007-09 Financial Crisis. Bear Stearns collapsed (Mar 2008), Lehman Brothers (Sep 2008). Great Recession. Unemployment hit 10%.

Austrian Perspective: Most egregious credit boom in US history. Greenspan/Bernanke kept rates at 1% (2003-04), inflated massive housing bubble. Securitization spread risk throughout financial system. Inversion was screaming warning - housing boom built on unsustainable credit expansion. Fed ignored it. Worst crisis since Depression vindicated Austrian business cycle theory.

?? Aug 2019 (2 days)

Context: Trade war tensions. Fed had raised rates to 2.5%. Repo market stress emerging. Manufacturing slowing.

What Happened: Brief inversion, then COVID-19 pandemic (2020) caused fastest recession in US history. Fed cut to zero, unlimited QE. Fiscal stimulus unprecedented.

Austrian Perspective: Flash inversion revealed fragility from decade of zero rates post-2008. Repo crisis (Sep 2019) showed banking system couldn't function without Fed intervention. COVID provided cover for monetary/fiscal explosion that would have occurred anyway to prevent debt deflation.

?? Jul 2022 - Sep 2024 (26 months)

Context: Inflation hit 9.1% (Jun 2022) - highest since 1981. Fed raised rates from 0% to 5.5% (fastest hiking cycle in history). SVB, Signature Bank collapsed (Mar 2023).

What Happened: Still unfolding (as of Jun 2026). Soft landing narrative vs. delayed recession debate. Commercial real estate stress. Regional bank failures contained. Inflation cooling but sticky.

Austrian Perspective: Inevitable consequence of 2020-21 monetary/fiscal explosion ($5T printed). Longest inversion since 1980s signals severe distortions from decade+ of near-zero rates (2008-2021). Zombification of corporate sector. Malinvestments everywhere (unprofitable tech, CRE overbuilding, overleveraged PE). Fed trapped: can't normalize rates without triggering crisis, but low rates fuel inflation/bubbles. Resolution likely requires significant recession to liquidate excesses.

Key Takeaway: Yield curve inversions historically precede recessions by 6-24 months. They signal that credit expansion has created unsustainable economic structures. Austrian theory explains inversions as the market recognizing that long-term investments funded by artificially cheap credit won't survive at normalized rates.
